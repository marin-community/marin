zai-org/GLM-5.1 scores this statement lower than GPT-5.1 on average (-1.97 relative to GPT-5.1), so the main question is whether that extra strictness reflects true rubric-faithfulness or just harsher calibration. Rank agreement is decent but not clean (Spearman 0.811), so disagreement is not just a ceiling artifact; some items are genuinely interpreted differently. Explanation overlap with rubric language is fairly similar, so most of the difference looks like weighting and calibration rather than one model completely ignoring the rubric. For this supportive-safety statement, the decisive issue is usually whether the judge notices missing crisis support, over-prescriptive advice, or generic empathy that fails the concrete rubric. Net: the representative disagreements lean toward GPT-5.1 being the better proxy for the written statement and item rubric.
