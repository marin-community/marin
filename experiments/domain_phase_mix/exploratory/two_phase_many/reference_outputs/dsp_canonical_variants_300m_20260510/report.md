# DSP Canonical Form Sweep on 300M

## Setup

DSP denotes the reduced-bias domain saturation-penalty family. The sweep keeps M-dependent
parameters to at most four per domain and uses only fixed global scalars for two-phase effects.

Compared variants:

- `dsp_no_phase_penalty_nnls`: Control: per-domain saturation and penalty with no phase term.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_penalty_nnls`: Canonical reduced-bias DSP: phase-1 premium only multiplies the benefit signal.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_exp_retention_penalty_nnls`: Canonical DSP plus original GRP-style exponential phase-0 retention.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_overlap_retention_penalty_nnls`: Canonical DSP plus a simple scalar overlap-retention exposure term.
- `dsp_effective_exposure_penalty_nnls`: Empirical comparator: phase-1 multiplier enters exposure for benefit and penalty.
- `dsp_effective_exposure_exp_retention_penalty_nnls`: Original GRP-style interpolation: retained phase-0 exposure plus phase-1 multiplier.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_saturation_penalty_nnls`: Decouples phase-1 benefit, saturation, and penalty multipliers as separate global scalars.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_saturation_nnls`: Canonical DSP plus a separate phase-1 saturation multiplier; penalty exposure remains phase-neutral.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_penalty_phase_nnls`: Canonical DSP plus a separate phase-1 penalty multiplier; saturation exposure remains phase-neutral.
- `dsp_saturation_only_penalty_nnls`: Tests whether phase effects are mainly faster saturation, without a benefit premium or penalty premium.
- `dsp_penalty_phase_only_nnls`: Tests whether phase effects are mainly faster phase-1 overexposure penalty.
- `dsp_saturation_penalty_split_nnls`: Unties effective exposure into separate saturation and penalty multipliers, with no benefit premium.
- `dsp_benefit_effective_exposure_nnls`: Canonical benefit premium plus one tied effective-exposure multiplier for saturation and penalty.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_penalty_signed`: Tests nonnegative-head assumption by using a signed ridge linear head.
- `dsp_phase_benefit_no_penalty_nnls`: Tests whether explicit overexposure penalties are needed.
- `dsp_effective_exposure_no_penalty_nnls`: Tests whether the empirical phase multiplier can replace explicit penalties.

## Interpretation

- Best CV RMSE row: `dsp_saturation_penalty_split_nnls` with cv_rmse=0.006407.
- Best OOF Spearman row: `dsp_saturation_penalty_split_nnls` with oof_spearman=0.929312.
- The original collaborator-facing canonical DSP form remains `dsp_phase_benefit_penalty_nnls`: phase-1 only changes the benefit amplitude, not saturation or penalty exposure.
- The strongest 300M fit is now `dsp_saturation_penalty_split_nnls`, which gives phase-1 separate global multipliers for saturation and penalty exposure and no benefit premium.
- Splitting effective exposure improves over the tied effective-exposure comparator: CV RMSE 0.006407 vs 0.007106, OOF Spearman 0.929312 vs 0.919645.
- Fully decoupled benefit/saturation/penalty also improves over tied effective exposure: CV RMSE 0.006635, OOF Spearman 0.926152. Its fitted benefit gamma is effectively zero (0.002044), so the data is not asking for a separate benefit premium once saturation and penalty phase effects are available.
- Adding only saturation to canonical helps modestly (CV RMSE 0.008287 vs canonical 0.008835); adding only phase-sensitive penalty helps more (CV RMSE 0.007511).
- Interpretation: the tied effective-exposure comparator was not just a numerical accident. In this fit, phase-1 curvature/penalty exposure carries real predictive signal. The less defensible part was tying all mechanisms to one scalar, not allowing saturation/penalty phase effects at all.
- Retention variants add one global nonlinear parameter. The exponential variant is closest to the original GRP retained-exposure interpolation; the overlap variant tests a simpler same-domain repeated-exposure bonus.
- Reduced-bias exponential retention improves train RMSE (0.004992 vs 0.005629) but worsens CV RMSE (0.011462 vs 0.008835); it looks like overfit rather than a real improvement.
- Simple overlap retention is nearly neutral: CV RMSE 0.008914 vs canonical 0.008835, with a tiny Spearman gain (0.900624 vs 0.898476). Its fitted overlap scalar is near zero, so it is not buying much.
- Original-style effective-exposure exponential retention also does not beat the existing effective-exposure comparator: CV RMSE 0.007320 vs 0.007106.
- Empirical upper-bound comparator: `dsp_effective_exposure_penalty_nnls`. It is now dominated by the split saturation/penalty variants, which are more diagnostic because they avoid forcing benefit, saturation, and penalty to share one phase scalar.
- Removing explicit penalties is not viable here: both no-penalty variants lose roughly 0.005-0.006 CV RMSE versus the canonical form and have worse top-row regret.
- Allowing signed heads does not help fit or rank; it weakens semantics and increases top-row regret versus NNLS.
- Use raw optima only as diagnostics until the off-manifold/collapse issue is fixed.
- Candidate next canonical DSP form should be chosen only after checking cross-scale stability and perturbation-gradient agreement. On 300M alone, `dsp_saturation_penalty_split_nnls` is the strongest reduced-bias form under the four-M-dependent-parameters-per-domain rule.
